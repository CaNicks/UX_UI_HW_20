Problem Statement

A Little Help was created for older adults to receive help doing the tasks that they can no longer accomplish themselves. However, users are put off by the long application form, potentially causing a decrease in volunteers.

Solution

The goal of this project was to rework the homepage and sign-up page for volunteers by breaking up information and adding more to allow the site to be more user friendly and to allow a user to know what A Little Helps mission is

My Role

UX Designer in a team of 4 designers Made Figma Components User Testing Making Surveys Wireframing Time Management

Reaserch

Initial Reaserch

The team and I starting our research by first contacting the stakeholder to set up a meeting. We wanted to get to know more about A Little Help since this was an outside project. We continued on by conducting a I Like, I Wish, What If test, before and after out initial round of testing. This allowed us to really delve into the site.

User Flow

With the main focus of the redesign being the Application we really wanted to define exactly how the user would get to the application. We wanted it to be something that ce be found in multiple places without requiring more than 3 clicks to start.

Survey and User Testing

After completing the Competitor Analysis and Initial Research we continued to conduct a survey focused around the user experience when it comes to filling out a form. We wanted to get a better idea of the frustrations they have and how we can avoid them.

Competitor Analysis

To get a better idea of how other services and companies assists older members of society, we completed a competitor analysis. This biggest discovery was that all the competitors would only come out to assist if certain criteria such as minimum amount of tasks, set time, or pay by task was meet. This caused many of A Little Helps members to lose out due to the high cost and requirements

Ideation

Survey and User Testing

We continued on with Ideation after we starting working through our prototypes. This happened from the Low Fidelity to the High Fidelity where we found things such as increasing font size even more to give the ideal reading experience, To adding auto fill to the form to even further increase the time to apply. We also made changes based off initial feedback to the low fidelity which will be touched on further down the slides.

Wireframes

For the Prototypes, our main focus was keeping the site as simple as possible to deliver the most information, and to have accessibility be the forefront of the site. The group split into groups of 2, one group mainly focused on the Homepage, while the other focused on the application form. I worked on the Application page where the main focus was getting the most information possible while keeping the sign up time to a minimum.

Design

Iterations

Throughout the prototyping process, the team wanted to keep the design as simple as possible, there were a few reason behind this decisions, The first being if they decided to use the prototype we made with a third party, or even make themselves we didn’t want it to be some thing that would required a full time developer to make. The second was the accessibility, since the site is targeted towards older members of society we didn’t want to have anything flashy or to difficult to navigate

Style Guide

To keep accessibility as a main focus, we chose to keep all the colors as either Complimentary or Analogous to not clash. For the text we wanted wide shaped letter without any fancy curves or groves to keep legibility high.

Donation Button

During the design process, the donation button that resided at the ned of the application for volunteers was a big point of contention for the team and the stakeholders. They were very skeptical to remove it despite the multiple user tests that gave remarks saying that it felt weird or inappropriate to ask for money before you even volunteered. This lead to having a few discussion with the stakeholders and after some back and forth agreed it could be changed.

Conclusion

Stakeholder Review

After completing the High Fidelity, we met with the Stakeholders for a second time to get their opinion on our work. The response was quite positive, they thought the minimized application made the process so much more seamless without compromising the information they were getting. While the homepage they though really gave a better understanding of what their mission was, as this was a big issues they saw was users couldn’t figure out what they did as a non profit. Overall they really liked the prototype and were willing to compromise on the donation button at the end of the application. Finally they offered to have us completely re-do the entire website if that was something we would be interested in. We are now currently in talks with them about the next steps.

Reflection & Next Steps

After meeting with the stakeholders we got back together to de brief on the meeting. We discussed how we still had some work to do on a few things but mainly how we worked well as a team, and were able to complete the project without any internal issues.
